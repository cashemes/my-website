About Me

Hi! My name is Christopher Shemes. I built this website so you could learn a little bit about me and what I'm trying to do. But before I can tell you what I want to do I need to introduce myself and tell you a little bit about myself and my life so far.

My name is Christopher Shemes and I'm a 16 year old boy on a mission. I've grown up loving exercise and the outdoors. Ever since I was little my parents have been taking me and my 4 siblings to National Parks. I love to hike. The beautiful views I've seen and the family conversations I've had on hikes make going to National Parks some of my best memories.

Now this was great, but something changed. In the Spring of 2020 the whole world flipped upside down. Covid 19 hit the planet like a wrecking ball. During this struggling time I was diagnosed with ADHD. This was now a life changing idea in my head. How was I going to manage? Was I the same person? Was I going to be okay? What did I need to do?

All of the answers to these questions came pretty quickly. It was hard to manage. I was the same person. I was okay. I needed to learn how to control my energy and manage my anger. These answers while straight forward were still very hard to understand as a small 10 year old boy. I didn't understand why I was different from my classmates and friends. Why couldn't I be normal?

When 5th grade started I had the chance to sign up for middle school sports. I loved playing soccer but we didn't have a middle school team. I wasn't sure I wanted to play any sports, so I didn't sign up for any. Long story short, my mom ended up signing me up for cross country without me knowing.

Now that might not sound like a horrible thing but for me it sounded awful. I had only run a few 5k's before with family and was not ready to start running for fun. My mom told me that running would help me get out my extra energy from my ADHD, but I wasn't so sure. Either way the next week my mom told me I had cross country practice.

I was not ready for this. I couldn't run. Who in the world enjoyed running and working hard? Not me! I cried and cried trying to convince my mom not to make me go to practice. She didn't listen. I was pushed into the car and told I was going. When we arrived at the high school my tears were just begining dry. I got out of the car scared and went to practice.

By the time I got home from practice I was a whole new person. That practice had transformed me from an energetic little devil to a determined little speed demon. I was changed. I had run 3 miles with my new teamates. Well actually in front of them. I didn't know how to pace myself, but I sure knew how to run fast. I loved the feeling of the wind behind my ears and the hard work it took.

Since that day I've continued running. I began improving in many parts of my life that had been rough before. Running was like a cure for my ADHD. It calmed me and let me hyperfocus on one thing that was important to me.

Now I'm 16. I've been to cross country states 2 times in two years of running high school cross country. I love workouts and especially long runs. But recently, I've wanted something more. I've been yearning for more mileage. My body has been begging me to run longer, farther, faster. I have an amazing coach who I respect very much but he won't let me run any farther because he needs me in shape to run track and cross country races, not marathons and ultras. But my body is getting angry with me. I need to do something about it. I need to run farther.

This is why I've made this website. I want to run farther. But I also want to set a big goal, not just endlessly run forever. So here is my goal: I want to run the Appalachian Trail. All 2,189 miles of it. I will run it southbound, meaning I start in the North and run South. While this will be a tough goal to reach, I know I can do it. I love running and I have the grit to keeping going. But this goal isn't possible without you.

Running the Appalachian Trail is an expensive journey. It requires a crew and lots of supplies. I can do the running, but I don't have $10,000 to do this. I need you to help me gain following on social media so that enough people will see me run that big brands will sponsor me and help me pay for the journey.

Now that you know a little about me and what I want to do, you can understand a little better how you can help me. One easy way is by donating a small amount towards my journey. Another way to help me is by sharing this website with a friend. They don't have to be a runner. They don't have to be athletic. They just need to read this and be inspired enough to help.

Thank you for reading this. I hope it was inspriring. I want to complete this goal to prove to others that setting the bar high doesn't mean that a goal is impossible, but that it just takes more work than normal. If you were inspired by my story and want to help please check out my How You Can Help page on this website to learn in more detail how to help me to complete this big goal.
